Remove me from the campus recruiting network

Why you might be here

Members of this network can upload their own contact export so the system can find people they already know. If someone you know did that and you were in their contacts, your name may have been used to derive a private connection — you don't have an account here, and nothing about you is shown to anyone.

You can ask for those derived records to be removed. Two ways, below. Neither one requires an account, and neither one tells us — or you — whether any record actually exists: we answer every request the same way on purpose, so this page can't be used to check who is in the network.

Confirm the address, then it's your own removal

We email that address a confirmation code. Entering it proves you control the address, which makes the removal your own action rather than an anonymous claim about someone else. The code expires in 48 hours.

Enter the code

The email also carries the request id, so you can come back and finish this on any device.

Ask a coordinator to review

Without a proven email address we can't treat this as your own action, so a school coordinator reviews it by hand. What you type here is held only while the review is open, is scrubbed once it's decided, and is deleted after 30 days either way.

If you do have an account here

This page is for people who don't. A member's own controls — delete my network, revoke discovery, erase my account — live on your own dashboard and do considerably more than this page can.
