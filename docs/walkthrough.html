The short version

What we built

Think of it as a private, smarter version of Handshake — the platform universities use to connect students with employers. We rebuilt it around two ideas most job sites miss:

1. An AI that reads resumes and job posts and ranks candidates against a role — and, crucially, explains its reasoning in plain English instead of a mystery score. 2. A relationship network — warm introductions, referrals, mentorship — built only from connections people choose to share. No scraping, no cold outreach. Around both, we built genuine fairness checks and strong privacy controls, because a hiring tool touches people's careers and their data.

Why it's different

Three ideas at the core

01

Matching you can trust

The AI scores each candidate against a job and shows why — the skills that matched, what's missing, where they'd need to grow. It flags resumes that try to game the system, and it never invents a fake "chance of getting hired."

02

Relationship-based hiring

The best jobs come through people you know. The platform finds a path from a student to a hiring manager through mutual connections and asks both sides before introducing them — plus mentorship, alumni, and verified references.

03

Fair & private by design

Bias is measured and flagged, never used to make decisions. Everything needs consent, people can delete their data in one step, and reports are built so no single individual can ever be picked out of a small group.

The relationship network

Built

Warm introductions

Finds a path from a student to a hiring manager through people they both know — and asks both sides before connecting them. Never a cold message.

How it worksStudent requests an intro on a job → a mutual connection is asked → if they and the manager both say yes, the intro is made.

Mentorship

Students opt in to find a mentor; alumni and professionals opt in to offer time. The platform matches them by program and interest.

How to useTurn on “looking for a mentor” → get matched → the mentor accepts before any contact happens.

Alumni network

Graduates keep a verified place in the network — the connection between “who’s hiring” and “who was once in your seat.”

How to useThe career centre confirms alumni status; alumni choose whether to mentor or take intros.

Event check-ins → connections

Checking in at a career fair or info session creates real, consented connections with the people who were actually there.

How to useScan in at the event → attendees you overlapped with become part of your network.

Verified references

A professor or manager vouches for a student. It becomes a portable, verifiable reference employers can see — stronger than a PDF letter.

How to useStudent sends a private request link → the referee writes a short structured vouch → the career centre can verify it.

Coordinator relationship dashboard

Career staff can see which students are under-connected — purely from network structure, never personal traits — and bridge them to a willing mentor in one click.

How to useOpen the dashboard → see the under-connected list → pick a mentor → Bridge.

Fairness & privacy

Built-in throughout

Consent for everything

Nothing about a person is shared or used without their explicit yes — and turning a consent off actually removes the data, not just hides it.

Bias is watched, never used

The system measures fairness across groups and flags problems for humans — but personal traits never touch a score or a ranking.

One-click data deletion

A person can delete their account and every trace of their data across the whole system, permanently.

Small-group protection

Reports never show a number small enough to identify an individual — a privacy guardrail on every summary.
